War Memorial Hall, Balcony Railing Safety Upgrades

Architect                      Raminder Kanetkar Architect Inc.
Project Architect                      Raminder Kanetkar
Year work completed                      2011
Project Value                      $60,000
Project Location                      Guelph, Ontario

Project Description:
Gross Floor Area: NA
This project involved safety upgrades for the balcony section of the War Memorial Hall at the University of Guelph. This Circa 1930 building is used for lectures, convocation and various ceremonies throughout the year. The existing wooden balcony front railing running approximately 50'-0" in length, and looking down approximately 10' was not built high enough to meet the Ontario Building Code (OBC).

Unique Design Features:
The balcony railing design was to provide safety and maintain the vision lines from the balcony seats to the center stage below. The solution was simple and elegant. A tempered glass extension was added above the existing wooden balcony rail. The vertical glass also provided the required height for the safety railing.

Challenges Unique to the Project:
Construction and attachment of the glass railing were designed with the help of the Structural consultant. The structural loading requirements were designed as per the OBC. Project also had to be completed on a tight budget and time schedule, just in time for the new semester start. The project was built on time and within budget. A building permit was also required from the City of Guelph

Project Involvement/Role:
Raminder Kanetkar Architect Inc. was responsible for the railing design, project approval from the authorities, Code analysis, Contract documents, and project sign off.

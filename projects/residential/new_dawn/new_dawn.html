New dawn SISCO Housing and Immigrant Centre Proposal

Architect                      McCallum Sather Architects (MSA)
Design Architect                      Raminder Kanetkar
Year work completed                      2006
Project Value                      Unknown
Project Location                      Project Not Constructed

Project Description:
Gross Floor Area: approx. 21,000sqft
This was a proposal for re-purposing of an existing school building in the Wentworth area of Hamilton into transitional housing, administration offices and short term accommodation for the new immigrants. The idea behind the project was to provide various services to the new immigrants, including housing, on one site. The design proposal included repurposing of the existing three storey school structure into administrative offices and short term hotel type accommodation for the new immigrants. The existing arena attached to the school was in a dilapidated shape. The arena building was to be demolished and replaced with eleven (11) traditional houses as transitional accommodation for the families of new immigrants, until they could find a permanent place to live.

Unique Design Features:
The plan arrangement for the new housing was aimed to foster social interaction and a feeling of community with design features such as central pedestrian access, shared front yards, and colourful visible entry doors to each unit. Housing units were designed to accommodate families with kids and were designed with familiar facade features found in many traditional houses in Canada. Some facade upgrades such as replacement of the existing windows and addition of the curtain wall to the administration area were proposed for the re-purposed buildings.

Challenges Unique to the Project:
There were significant budget limitations on this project.

Project Involvement/Role:
As Design Architect Raminder was responsible for the proposal design from start to finish.

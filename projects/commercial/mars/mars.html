MaRS CENTRE PARCEL B

Architect                      Adamson Associates Architects (AAA)
Partner-in-Charge                      David Jensen (AAA)
Project Architect                      Irina Farb
Job Captain                      Raminder Kanetkar
Year work completed                      2007
Project Value                      Unknown
Project Location                      Toronto, Ontario

Project Description:
Gross Floor Area: approx. 150,000 sq ft on 5 floors
MaRs parcel 'B' comprises of the five storey high above ground Heritage building facing College Street and an Eight storey high tower behind it. MaRs Parcel 'B' is flanked by Parcel 'A' tower towards Avenue road and Parcel 'C' high rise towards Elizabeth Street. Parcel 'B' also includes a three storey high basement comprising of parking, a lower concourse and an upper concourse. A unique mixed use building for a combination of functions such as medical Labs, Law offices & shopping, won an OAA award of excellence in the year 2010 for exemplanary preservation and enhancement of the salient heritage features of the original Toronto General Hospital building. Redevelopment of College wing is a prime example of an adaptive reuse. A concrete and steel structure for the new towers along with curtain wall facades and existing restored concrete structure with brick facade for the heritage wing.

Unique Design Features:
This project had various unique aspects in its exterior as well as interior design some of which have been discussed below:

  • A handsomely designed exterior landscaped forecourt with new ramps for accessibility provide a unique and formal entrance setting.
  • Carefully restored windows and brick facade add a flair of elegance and provide a handsome foreground and human scale to the high rise towers on all sides.
  • Glazed skylight linkages create well lit dynamic interactive spaces.
  • Behind the heritage facade, the Interior spaces are contemporary and well lit.
Challenges Unique to the Project:
This was a unique project that was very large in its scale and budget. Co-ordination of all consultants and a dynamic team work were some of the unique challenges of this project.

Project Involvement/Role:
From the project Inception to the Tender stage involved in the capacity of Job Captain with:
  • The design development
  • Interior & exterior building details
  • OBC co-ordination with the code consultant
  • Accessibility compliance for entrance and washrooms
  • Production and co-ordination of drawings
  • consultant co-ordination
